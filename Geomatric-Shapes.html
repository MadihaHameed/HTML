Explaination of each point.

  1. The points attribute defines the vertices (points) of the polygon that forms the star shape. Each point is specified as a pair of coordinates, separated by a comma, with the x-coordinate first and the y-coordinate second.
  2. Here's a breakdown of the points for this particular star shape:
    1. (50,0) - Top center point of the star
    2. (63,38) - Upper-right point of the star
    3. (100,38) - Upper-right point of the star
    4. (69,63) - Lower-right point of the star
    5. (82,100) - Bottom center point of the star
    6. (50,75) - Lower-left point of the star
    7. (18,100) - Bottom left point of the star
    8. (31,63) - Lower-left point of the star
    9. (0,38) - Upper-left point of the star
    10. (37,38) - Upper-left point of the star
  3. The points are connected in order to create the shape of the star.
  4. In the context of the points attribute for an SVG polygon, (50,0) refers to a point with an x-coordinate of 50 and a y-coordinate of 0.
    1. In other words, (50,0) is a coordinate pair that represents a point on the SVG canvas, where the x-coordinate is 50 units from the left edge of the canvas and the y-coordinate is 0 units from the top edge of the canvas.
    2. So in the case of the star shape, (50,0) is the top center point of the star, with an x-coordinate of 50 and a y-coordinate of 0.
